Study on Chemical Kinetic Characteristics and Electrochemical-Thermal-Kinetic Interaction Mechanism of NCM Lithium-ion Battery Thermal Runaway Process

The variations of gas generation rate with respect to (a) temperature and (b) heat generation rate caused by side reactions curves of six cells with different charging procedures.

Overview Link to heading

Lithium-ion batteries are prone to thermal runaway under high-temperature abuse and overcharging conditions. This study investigates thermal runaway behaviors in 18650-type cells by combining accelerating rate calorimetry with a battery testing system. The focus is on constant current constant voltage (CCCV) charging at rates of 0.2 C, 0.5 C, 1 C, and 2 C.

The findings reveal that cells subjected to CCCV charging at high temperatures are more likely to experience thermal runaway. Higher charging rates result in increased irreversible heat and promoted side reactions, leading to more severe thermal runaway events and enhanced heat and gas generation. Additionally, the total heat and gas production show a nonlinear relationship with the charging rate due to the combined effects of state of charge and charging current on side reactions.

After charging, the relationship between gas generation rate and heat generation rate can be characterized by two distinct linear growth stages. In the initial stage, gas generation rates remain consistent despite variations in charging rates, while in the subsequent stage, different gas generation rates are observed with increasing charging rates, indicating changes in side reaction pathways.
