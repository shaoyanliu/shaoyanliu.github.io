2019 FSEC in Zhuhai, captured by the official photographer – I’m the one pushing the car.

My Journey with the BJTU Formula Student Team Link to heading

Being a member of the BJTU Formula Student Team and participating in the Formula Student Electric China (FSEC, what’s this?) occupied a significant portion of my undergraduate years. I joined the team as a freshman, diving into the 2019 season as a member of the suspension group. My main responsibilities included the design, processing, and assembly of the car’s suspension system. Specifically, I worked on the push-rod double wishbone independent suspension, which, for the first time in our team’s history, featured a Z-shaped anti-roll bar in the rear suspension. My role involved designing the suspension geometry and structural components, creating engineering drawings, and assembling parts. I also traveled with the team to Zhuhai for the 2019 FSEC, where we earned the national “Excellence Award.”

In the 2020 season, I advanced to the role of chassis group leader, overseeing the coordination of the steering, braking, suspension, and transmission sub-teams. Building on the previous season’s progress, I focused on optimizing the structure across these systems. My responsibilities included liaising with sub-team leaders, coordinating solutions to design challenges, and managing the overall design and production timeline for the chassis team.

Throughout these two seasons, I evolved from a team member into a leader, capable of handling critical aspects of the project. The late nights spent working on the car remain some of the most memorable experiences of my life.

“Xiaoge, the team captain, said Shaoyan is strong, so he always asked me to help push the car."

“The people who stayed until the end became trusted friends.”

– Written on the night after the 2019 competition, on the train back to Beijing.
