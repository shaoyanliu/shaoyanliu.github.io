Safety Performance Assessment and Explosion Threshold Simulation of Lithium-ion Batteries under Electro-Thermal Coupled Abusive Conditions

Figure 1. Effects of minor generated gas components addition on the explosion limits of stoichiometric NCM cell generated gas/oxygen mixtures.

Overview Link to heading

This project investigates the gas generation characteristics and explosion limits of gases produced by 18650-type NCM cells during thermal runaway (TR) at various states of charge (SOCs). To initiate TR, an accelerating rate calorimeter is employed, and gas measurements are conducted using an airtight jar.

Besides, the project involves calculating the explosion limits of the generated gases and comparing these with those from LFP and NCA cells. It is found that NCM cells with lower SOCs exhibit slightly higher gas generation rates before violent TR, which is attributed to elevated reaction temperatures. During violent TR, the gas generation rate curves display multiple peaks, particularly under high SOC conditions. Additionally, the project observes typical Z-shaped explosion limit curves for all gas mixtures. Notably, as the cathode material transitions from NCM to LFP and NCA, the explosion limits shift from high-temperature to low-temperature regions.

In contrast to common beliefs, the project reveals non-monotonic responses in explosion limit curves with respect to cell SOC. Sensitivity analyses underscore the importance of minor gas components, such as ethylene and ethane, in reducing the explosivity of the gas mixture.

Contributions Link to heading
